Everyone knows that if you query poets about how their poems begin, the answer is always the same:

a phrase,
a line,
a scrap
of language,
a rhythm,
an image,
something seen,
heard,
witnessed,
or imagined.

And the lesson is always the same,

and young poets recognize this to be one of the most important lessons they can learn:

if you have any idea for a poem, an exact grid of intent

the wrong path, a dead-end alley, at the top of a cliff you haven’t even climbed. This is a lesson that can only be learned by trial and error.
